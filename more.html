4 Amazing Facts
About Cotton Candy

Fairy Floss

The cotton candy making process was developed by four men, named Thomas Patton, Josef Delarose Lascaux, John C. Wharton, and William Morrison in the year 1899. The original name for the treat was "Fairy Floss!"

Link

Dentists???

Believe it or not, John C. Wharton and William Morrison (two of the original cotton candy inventors) were dentists! Another man from New Orleans, Dr. Josef Lascaux, trademarked the name "Cotton Candy." He was a dentist as well.

Link

First Appearance

The first appearance of the treat was at the St. Louis World Fair in 1904. Each box sold for 25 cents, which at the time would have been equivelent to about 6 dollars now! That was half the price for an admission ticket at that particular fair.

Link

Unchanged Process

Overall, the process to make cotton candy hasn't changed much at all. Innovations have been brought to life, but the process is so simple that changing it would be impossible. Even the machines used today are generally the same as the ones used back then.
